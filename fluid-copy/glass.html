NYU Abu Dhabi Study Away | January to May

Academic Expansion + Early Research Exposure

I studied at NYU Abu Dhabi for one semester and took Linear Algebra, Ordinary Differential Equations, Electricity and Magnetism, Computational Physics, Futsal, and a research assistantship in the Astroparticle Physics Lab.

Living in the UAE expanded my cultural perspective, and the coursework changed my technical direction. Computational Physics introduced me to simulation-driven research, while the lab experience gave me direct insight into what an academic research career looks like day to day.

Vietnam Summer Program (Hanoi) | Digital Twin Project

Sustainable Cities Through Simulation

In Hanoi, I worked on a digital twin project focused on city traffic and urban sustainability, aligned with UN SDG 11. The program also included historical and linguistic study that deepened our understanding of Vietnamese culture.

This experience connected two interests I previously kept separate: data and sustainability. Using math and physics-based simulation to approach social problems showed me how technical tools can directly support public wellbeing.

Hanyang University International Winter School | Seoul

UN SDGs + Social Entrepreneurship in Korea

At Hanyang University, I studied basic Korean and a course on the UN Sustainable Development Goals. Language learning and field visits helped me understand Korean social values and current development priorities.

Visits to Hisbeans, MYSC, and Donghwa made social entrepreneurship tangible. Seeing disability-inclusive hiring, mission-driven incubation, and values-based business in practice broadened how I evaluate impact in both nonprofit and private sectors.

What GLASS Means in My Work

These experiences continue to shape how I choose projects: I look for work where technical depth and social impact are both present. Whether I am building optimization tools or analyzing product data, I try to keep global context and human outcomes visible in the process.
